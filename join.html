What does being a staff member entail?

Jabberwocky meets once a week to discuss the submissions. At the beginning of each week, you will receive a folder on Google Drive with submissions for the committee you are a part of. Your job is to read and analyze those submissions, and bring any ideas/notes you have on why each submission should or should not be included in the journal.

What are the different roles within Jabberwocky?

There are four staffs you could be a part of: poetry, fiction, media, or nonfiction/criticism. If you’re feeling you want more responsibility, you can apply to be a head editor which would require you to be in charge of leading the discussions for that staff.

What happens after all submissions are chosen?

Once we stop receiving submissions, if selected you can help copy edit the journal and design the layout! After it’s all complete and printed, we celebrate with a launch party for all staff and authors to attend!

Why should I join Jabberwocky?

Being a part of Jabberwocky is a very rewarding expereince. You will gain editing skills, collaboration skills, analyzation skills, as well as become part of a team dedicated to spreading the love of literature and art at UMass. Each printed journal is a reminder of the hardwork you have done and a reminder of the friendships you have made by being a part of the Jabberwocky community.
